Music 209 Course Calendar

Music 209 : Calendar

The table below shows the lecture dates for the course. For each lecture, we provide slides and a lecture outline. The lecture outline is annotated with references and web links for further study.

Week Date Title Slides Outline
1 Jan 19 Overview. Introduction to concatenative synthesis. Details on the course project. [pdf] [link]
2 Jan 26 Splicing. Evaluating the quality of a candidate concatenation, by comparing pitch, loudness, spectrum, and volume across the concatenation. Methods for performing splices in the time domain and the spectral domain (morphing). Guest appearance by Eric Lindemann of Synful [pdf] [link]
3 Feb 2 Speech Synthesis. Guest lecture on concatenative speech synthesis. Concatenative music techniques that are patterned after speech synthesis (diphone synthesis). [pdf] [link]
4 Feb 9 Time-Warping. Concatenation of two rhythmic units may require the tempos to match. We describe techniques for matching tempos without otherwise altering units. [pdf] [link]
5 Feb 16 Pitch-Shifting, Spectral Matching, Formants Matching. Techniques for altering one sample to match another in preparation for a splice. [pdf] [link]
6 Feb 23 Real-time Note Concatenation Control. Techniques for real-time note-level control, such as a MIDI hardware controller. [pdf] [link]
7 Mar 1 Project Abstracts Due (via email, Mar 1 is not a lecture day). See link for details. [link]
7 Mar 2 Database Descriptors. Methods to compute and use descriptors for database entries (spectral shape, loudness, pitch, etc). [pdf] [link]
8 Mar 9 Off-line Note Concatenation Control. Techniques for synthesizing audio from score notation or guide audio. [pdf] [link]
9 Mar 16 Spectral Methods. How to use compute spectral representations, and use them to perform signal processing tasks in concatenative synthesis. (David away) [pdf] [link]
